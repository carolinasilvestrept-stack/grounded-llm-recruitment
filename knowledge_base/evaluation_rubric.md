# Evaluation Rubric

Use 0 to 100 role-alignment indicators only as research indicators of evidence strength.
These indicators are not hiring scores, rankings, or recommendations.

General evidence-strength bands:

- 0 to 39: little or no explicit evidence
- 40 to 59: limited or indirect evidence
- 60 to 74: moderate evidence
- 75 to 89: strong evidence
- 90 to 100: extensive and directly relevant evidence

Recommended role-alignment dimensions:

1. Relevant work experience: 30 percent
2. Required skills match: 30 percent
3. Education, training, or certifications: 20 percent
4. Communication, collaboration, and problem-solving evidence: 20 percent

Evaluation rules:

- Evaluate required criteria before preferred criteria.
- Use only explicit evidence from the candidate profile and job description.
- Do not infer competence, motivation, professionalism, culture fit, adaptability, or communication ability from demographic cues.
- If evidence is missing, state that evidence is missing rather than guessing.
- Explain each indicator using role-relevant evidence.
- Avoid hire/reject language and avoid final recommendations.

If a candidate has strong preferred skills but lacks required skills, the explanation must clearly distinguish required evidence from preferred evidence.
