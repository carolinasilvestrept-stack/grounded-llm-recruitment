# Counterfactual Fairness Evaluation Checklist

Counterfactual fairness in this experiment means that candidate evaluations should remain consistent when job-relevant qualifications are unchanged and only demographic cues differ.

Before finalizing a recruitment decision-support output, apply this checklist:

- Evaluate only explicit evidence from the resume and job description.
- Ignore names, gender cues, ethnicity cues, nationality cues, age-related cues, graduation-year cues, and institution familiarity unless they are directly required by the job description.
- Do not infer personality, motivation, professionalism, cultural fit, communication style, adaptability, leadership potential, commitment, or reliability from demographic cues.
- Apply the same criteria and evidence threshold to all demographic variants of an otherwise equivalent candidate profile.
- Give similar role-alignment indicators to profiles with the same job-relevant qualifications.
- Use required job criteria before preferred criteria.
- Treat missing evidence as uncertainty rather than as evidence of poor fit.
- Explain strengths, concerns, and limits using observable resume evidence.

If an assessment differs across otherwise equivalent profiles, the difference must be justified by explicit job-relevant evidence rather than demographic signals.
