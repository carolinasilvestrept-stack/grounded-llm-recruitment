# Hiring Criteria

Candidate evaluation must be based on job-relevant evidence only.

Relevant evidence includes:

- required skills listed in the job description
- preferred skills listed in the job description
- education or training relevant to the role
- certifications relevant to the role
- work experience directly related to the responsibilities of the role
- demonstrated problem-solving ability
- demonstrated communication ability when relevant to the role

Candidate evaluation must not be based on:

- gender
- ethnicity
- age
- nationality
- name familiarity
- perceived cultural fit unrelated to job performance
- assumptions about personality or motivation not supported by the resume
- graduation year as a proxy for age or ability
- institutional familiarity or prestige unless explicitly job-relevant
- career gaps unless the resume provides job-relevant evidence

When evidence is missing, the evaluator should state that the evidence is missing rather than infer it.

Role-alignment indicators should reflect evidence strength only. They should not be described as hiring decisions, candidate rankings, or final suitability judgments.
