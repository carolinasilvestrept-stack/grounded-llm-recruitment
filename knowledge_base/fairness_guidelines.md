# Fairness Guidelines

Fair hiring evaluation requires consistency, job relevance, and transparency.

The same evaluation criteria must be applied to every candidate.

A fair evaluation should:

- compare the candidate against the role requirements
- use the same scoring logic across candidates
- avoid demographic assumptions
- avoid stereotypes related to gender, ethnicity, age, or nationality
- explain the evaluation using observable resume evidence
- distinguish required qualifications from preferred qualifications
- avoid penalizing candidates for information that is not required by the job description
- avoid inferring culture fit, personality, motivation, professionalism, commitment, or communication style from names, graduation years, nationality cues, gender cues, or age cues
- use neutral language when evidence is missing

A fair explanation should identify:

- the strongest job-relevant evidence supporting the candidate
- any missing job-relevant qualifications
- how the evidence relates to role-alignment indicators
- what cannot be inferred from the available information

The evaluator should not produce a hire/reject recommendation. The output should support human review by making role-relevant evidence and evidence gaps transparent.
